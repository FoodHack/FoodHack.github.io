Coffee Fertiliser

I came up with this trick when trying to work out the problem of coffee grounds and liquid building up in the bottom of the bin. After a little research, I found out that my mum wasn't crazy after all and there was a bona fide reason for using coffee as fertiliser. Apparently as the coffee degrades, it releases nitrogen into the soil which is crucial for most plants but particularly plants that bear fruit such as the lime tree above. If you're someone who drinks a formidable amount of coffee like me and also has a laissez faire attitude towards maintaining your plants like I do, this is the trick for you. Instead of throwing your used coffee grounds in the bin distrubute them on the soil of any plants you may have. Then dig them into the soil a little. I usually store a week's worth in tupperware in the fridge and do it in bulk.
