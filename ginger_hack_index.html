Grow Your Own

My friends Rod and Kris told me about this trick which is easy as and also pretty low maintenance. All you need to do is get a piece of ginger from your local greengrocer or supermarket. Soak it overnight in a bowl of water and then stick it in a pot with some potting soil with the top part of the rhizome about a centimetre below the surface of the soil. It needs a little batural sunlight each day but no too much. Two hours should do it. It's very important to keep it watered. It will seem like nothing is happening for a while until eventually some green stems will start to grow. When the stems look like they have peaked you can remove the rhizome which will now be larger. You can break it down into smaller pieces and repeat the process or you can stop being a smartypants and just use the ginger!
This trick also works with spring onions according to my friend Fi. Who says you just retain the tips of the spring onions after use and sow them in rows in a long pot about 20cm apart. This is my next experiment!
